# Green America Style Guide

## Who We Are

Green America is a crew of climbers, cutters, and craftsmen who do dangerous, skilled work so your property stays safe and beautiful. We're not a "green" company in the Whole Foods sense. We're green because that's what we work in — canopy, all day, every day.

This is blue-collar excellence. The kind of work where your office is 40 feet in the air and your desk is a limb.

---

## Brand Position

**Not a tree service. A craft.**

Green America sits at the intersection of skilled trades and lifestyle. We attract clients who respect craftsmanship — people who want to know the crew showing up actually gives a damn. And we attract crew members who see this as more than a paycheck.

We are NOT:
- An environmental brand
- A "green living" company
- A corporate landscaping outfit
- Trying to save the planet (we're trying to save your roof from that dead oak)

We ARE:
- Skilled tradespeople who happen to work in trees
- A crew you'd want to ride along with
- The kind of company where the owner still climbs
- Atlanta's most interesting tree company

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Principles

1. **Talk like a person, not a brochure.** "We'll get that dead limb down before it gets your car" beats "Our certified arborists provide hazard mitigation services."

2. **Confident, not cocky.** We know what we're doing. We don't need to tell you seventeen times.

3. **Dry humor welcome.** A little wit goes a long way. We're not a funeral home.

4. **Short sentences hit harder.** Don't explain what you can show.

5. **Respect the work.** This job is dangerous. People get hurt. We take the craft seriously even when the tone is casual.

### Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Marketing / Social | Confident, witty, a little swagger |
| Emergency / Storm | Direct, calm, no-nonsense, reassuring |
| Educational | Knowledgeable but approachable |
| Hiring / Crew | Real talk, no corporate fluff |

### Words We Use
- Crew (not team or staff)
- Climbers (not technicians)
- The cut, the climb, the job
- Craft, trade, skill
- Ridealong (one word, our thing)

### Words We Don't Use
- Eco-friendly, sustainable, green (as in environmental)
- Solutions, synergy, leverage
- "Your tree care partner"
- Anything that sounds like it came from a template

## Visual Direction

### Photography
- Real crew, real jobs. No stock photos. Ever.
- Show the height. Show the saws. Show the sweat.
- Golden hour shots of canopy work = money
- Crew portraits: gear on, not posed
- Before/after splits for results

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Deep forest green (#1B4332) — not bright, not neon. Dark, like shade under a canopy.
- **Secondary:** Worn khaki/tan (#C9B99A) — like Carhartt after a week of work
- **Accent:** Chainsaw orange (#E85D04) — safety gear, bar oil caps, high-vis
- **Dark:** Near-black (#0B1D0F) — for text, dark backgrounds
- **Light:** Off-white (#F5F1EB) — warm, not sterile

### Typography Direction
- Headlines: Bold, condensed, industrial. Think work truck lettering.
- Body: Clean, readable, no-nonsense. Nothing decorative.
- All-caps sparingly — for emphasis, not shouting.

### Logo Direction
- Should work on a truck door, a hat, and a website
- No leaves, no globes, no recycling arrows
- Think: badge, mark, stamp — something a crew would wear
- The kind of logo that looks good as a patch on a Carhartt jacket

## The Ridealong

This is our secret weapon. Nobody else does this.

**What it is:** We invite potential clients (and anyone curious) to ride along on a job. Free. No obligation. Come watch a climber work 40 feet up with a gas chainsaw. See the rigging. Hear the saws. Understand why this costs what it costs.

**Why it works:**
- Kills price objections instantly (you see the skill and risk involved)
- Creates stories people tell their friends
- Builds trust before you ever sign anything
- Content machine — every ridealong is a potential video, photo, testimonial

**How we talk about it:**
> "Never hired a tree crew before? Come ride along. See what we do from the ground — or closer, if you're brave."

## Storm Response Brand

When severe weather hits, Green America shifts into a different gear. The tone changes. The pace changes. This gets its own visual treatment.

- Dedicated landing pages per storm event
- Real-time updates (weather tracking, crew deployment)
- SEO-optimized for "[storm name] + tree damage + Atlanta"
- Tone: calm authority. "We're already out there."
- Same-day service messaging front and center

## Social Media Voice

- Instagram: The visual craft. Climbing shots, canopy views, crew life.
- TikTok: The "holy shit" moments. Drops, cuts, close calls (safely), ridealongs.
- Facebook: Community. Storm updates, crew spotlights, local jobs.
- All platforms: Talk like a human. Reply like a human. Be the company people actually want to follow.
