Storm Helene: What to Expect in Atlanta

Storms like Helene cause predictable damage across metro Atlanta. Here's what we see most — and what to do if it happens to you.

🌳 [Photo: Uprooted Tree]

Uprooted Trees

Saturated soil + high winds = root failure. Large trees can topple onto homes, vehicles, and power lines. Do not approach — call us.

🏠 [Photo: Limb on Roof]

Limbs on Structures

Heavy branches punching through rooflines. If you hear impact overhead, move to an interior room and call for emergency removal.

🔌 [Photo: Tree on Power Line]

Trees on Power Lines

Extremely dangerous. Stay at least 35 feet away. Call Georgia Power first (888-891-0938), then call us for the tree.

🚗 [Photo: Blocked Driveway]

Blocked Roads & Driveways

Fallen trees and debris block access. We clear driveways, roads, and property access points so you can get in and out safely.

⚡ [Photo: Hanging Limb]

Hanging / Widow-Maker Limbs

Partially broken branches lodged in the canopy. They can fall without warning. Don't walk under them — call for professional removal.

🏡 [Photo: Fence Damage]

Fence & Property Damage

Trees and heavy limbs crush fences, sheds, and outbuildings. We remove the tree and haul the debris so your contractor can rebuild.

⚠️ Safety While You Wait

  • Stay away from downed trees and power lines — assume all lines are live.
  • Don't climb on your roof to assess damage. Take photos from the ground.
  • If a tree is leaning on your house, evacuate that side of the home.
  • Document everything with photos and video for your insurance claim.
  • Keep our number handy: (770) 560-8656 — we answer 24/7 during storms.
